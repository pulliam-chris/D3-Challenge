2014 1-Year ACS Estimates from the USCB

Exploring Trends Between Poverty, Healthcare, Smoking and Obsesity

With regards to the estimates of Poverty level within a state, there appears to be a positive correlation in the relative levels of poverty to being more likely to lack healthcare and have a populace more prone to smoking.

There also appears to be positive correlations between states that have higher relative levels of the populace are classified as obese to higher levels of smoking and lacking healthcare within their state.
